Build-a-Board: Chess Meta Visualization

In this project, we present a visualization of which pieces are moved most often at different points during a chess game, and to which rows, columns, and cells they are most frequently moved. We also allow these results to be filtered by player level and color. We invite you to play around with the following options.

♚♛♜

Step 1: Select what data to analyze:

Data on individual chess pieces (King, Queen, Knight, Rook, etc...)

Data for rows (1, 2, 3, ..., 8)

Data for columns (a, b, c, ..., h)

Data for destinations on the chess board (a1, a2, a3, ..., h8)

Step 2: Select graph type:

Display y-axis data normalized from 0 to 1

Display y-axis data

Display y-axis data as a line connecting peaks
